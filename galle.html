Overview

The Old Town of Galle and its fortifications represent the fortified city built by the Portuguese in 1588 on the Galle headland and extensively fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century from 1649 onwards.

Galle Fort is a perfect example of a fortified city built by Europeans in South and Southeast Asia, showing the interaction between European architectural styles and South Asian traditions. The 36-hectare fort area contains streets of Dutch-colonial style houses, administrative buildings, churches, and commercial warehouses.

Today, Galle Fort remains a living heritage site with a vibrant community, boutique hotels, restaurants, shops, and art galleries nestled within its historic ramparts.

Historical Significance

Portuguese Period (1588-1640)

The Portuguese first built a small fort called 'Santa Cruz' after they conquered the Kingdom of Kotte. This initial fortification was relatively simple, with earthen walls and wooden palisades.

Dutch Period (1640-1796)

The Dutch captured Galle in 1640 and extensively fortified it with massive ramparts, bastions, and a grid pattern of streets. They built the iconic lighthouse, Dutch Reformed Church, and numerous colonial buildings that still stand today.

British Period (1796-1948)

Under British rule, Galle continued to be an important port city. The British added their own architectural elements, including the clock tower and made various administrative modifications.

Key Attractions

Galle Lighthouse

Built in 1938, standing 26.5 meters tall at the southern tip of the Fort, offering stunning ocean views.

Dutch Reformed Church

Built in 1755, features beautiful floor tiles from the Dutch East India Company and a historic pipe organ.

Fort Ramparts

14 bastions connected by walls, perfect for sunset walks with panoramic views of the Indian Ocean.

National Maritime Museum

Housed in a Dutch warehouse, displays maritime history and artifacts from shipwrecks.

Old Gate & Clock Tower

The main entrance with British coat of arms and the iconic clock tower built in 1883.

Church Street & Pedlar Street

Historic streets lined with colonial buildings, boutiques, cafes, and art galleries.

Architectural Features

Galle Fort showcases a unique blend of European and South Asian architectural styles:

  • Dutch Colonial Buildings: Thick walls, high ceilings, courtyards, and distinctive tile roofs
  • Fortification System: 14 bastions (Akersloot, Aurora, Neptune, etc.) connected by ramparts
  • Grid Street Pattern: Organized Dutch urban planning still intact today
  • Tropical Adaptation: Wide verandas, ventilation systems, and courtyards for tropical climate
