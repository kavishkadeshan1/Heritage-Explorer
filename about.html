About Heritage Explorer

Preserving Sri Lanka's Cultural Legacy for Future Generations

Our Mission

Heritage Explorer is dedicated to preserving, promoting, and educating people about Sri Lanka's magnificent cultural and natural heritage sites.

We believe that understanding our past is essential for building a better future. Through our platform, we aim to make Sri Lanka's incredible heritage accessible to everyone—from students and researchers to tourists and heritage enthusiasts.

Our comprehensive guides, stunning galleries, and interactive features help visitors discover the stories, architecture, and cultural significance behind each UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Our Vision & Values

Vision

To become the leading digital platform for exploring and learning about Sri Lanka's cultural heritage, inspiring global appreciation and conservation.

Preservation

We are committed to documenting and preserving the cultural heritage of Sri Lanka through accurate information and high-quality visual content.

Education

We believe in making heritage education accessible, engaging, and interactive for learners of all ages through modern digital tools.

Sri Lanka's UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Sri Lanka is home to 8 UNESCO World Heritage Sites—6 cultural and 2 natural

Ancient City of Polonnaruwa

Medieval capital with Buddhist temples and royal palaces (11th-12th century)

Ancient City of Sigiriya

5th-century rock fortress with advanced hydraulic systems

Sacred City of Anuradhapura

First ancient capital with the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree

Old Town of Galle

17th-century Dutch colonial fortress

Sacred City of Kandy

Last capital of Sinhalese kingdoms, home to the Temple of the Tooth

Golden Temple of Dambulla

Cave temple complex with ancient Buddhist murals

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

UNESCO Natural Heritage - Tropical rainforest biodiversity hotspot

Central Highlands

UNESCO Natural Heritage - Peak Wilderness, Horton Plains, Knuckles Range
