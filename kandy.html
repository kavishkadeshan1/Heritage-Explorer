The Last Royal Capital

Kandy was the last capital of the Sinhalese kings of Sri Lanka and served as the kingdom's administrative and religious center from 1592 to 1815 when it fell to the British.

The city is renowned worldwide as the home of the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in the world. According to tradition, possession of the Tooth Relic signified sovereignty, making Kandy the spiritual and political heart of the island.

Nestled among mountains at an elevation of 500 meters, Kandy enjoys a cooler climate and is surrounded by lush hills, tropical forests, and the scenic Kandy Lake. The city retains its historic charm with colonial architecture, Buddhist monasteries, and traditional cultural practices still thriving today.

Historical Significance

Kingdom of Kandy (1469-1815)

Kandy was the last independent kingdom of Sri Lanka, successfully resisting Portuguese and Dutch colonization for over 300 years. The kingdom maintained its sovereignty through strategic location in mountainous terrain.

The Sacred Tooth Relic

The tooth relic was brought to Sri Lanka in the 4th century AD, hidden in the hair of a princess. It has been venerated by Buddhists for over 1,600 years and is considered the most important religious object in Sri Lanka.

British Era (1815-1948)

The Kingdom of Kandy fell to the British in 1815 through the Kandyan Convention. The British preserved the Temple of the Tooth and respected local customs, allowing Kandy to retain its cultural significance.

Living Heritage

Today, Kandy remains a living sacred city. The Temple of the Tooth continues daily rituals, and the annual Esala Perahera festival attracts hundreds of thousands of pilgrims and tourists.

Must-Visit Sites

Temple of the Tooth

Sri Dalada Maligawa houses the sacred tooth relic. Daily rituals (puja) at dawn, noon, and evening allow devotees to pay respects.

Kandy Lake

Artificial lake built by King Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe in 1807. Scenic walkway around the lake offers stunning views.

Royal Palace Complex

Former residence of Kandyan kings, now houses museums displaying royal regalia, artifacts, and historical documents.

Royal Botanical Gardens

Peradeniya Gardens, 147 acres of tropical plants, orchids, and historic trees planted by world leaders.

Buddhist Temples

Lankatilaka, Embekke, and Gadaladeniya temples showcase Kandyan architecture and ancient wood carvings.

Kandy Viewpoint

Arthur's Seat offers panoramic views of Kandy city, the lake, and surrounding mountain ranges.

Esala Perahera Festival

One of Asia's Greatest Festivals

The Esala Perahera is held annually in July/August and lasts for 10 days. This grand procession celebrates the sacred tooth relic with:

  • 100+ Decorated Elephants: Led by the Maligawa Tusker carrying the sacred casket
  • Traditional Dancers: Kandyan dancers, fire dancers, and whip crackers
  • Drummers & Musicians: Traditional percussion ensembles with ancient instruments
  • Torch Bearers: Thousands of torches illuminate the night procession

Tip: Book accommodations months in advance during Perahera season as the city fills with pilgrims and tourists.

Cultural Heritage

Kandy preserves unique cultural traditions:

  • Kandyan Dance: Traditional dance form featuring acrobatic movements and elaborate costumes
  • Drumming Traditions: Complex rhythmic patterns on traditional geta beraya drums
  • Wood Carving: Intricate carvings on temple pillars and traditional buildings
  • Living Religious Practices: Daily rituals and ceremonies continue unchanged for centuries
