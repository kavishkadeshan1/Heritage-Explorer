Sacred City of Anuradhapura

Ancient Capital · Sri Maha Bodhi · UNESCO World Heritage Site

Historical Timeline

Foundation (377 BC)

King Pandukabhaya established Anuradhapura as the capital city. He planned the city with cemeteries, temples, hospitals, and parks.

Buddhism Arrives (3rd Century BC)

King Devanampiya Tissa received Buddhism from Mahinda (son of Emperor Ashoka). The Sri Maha Bodhi tree was planted from a sapling of the original Bodhi tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment.

Golden Age (1st-5th Century AD)

Construction of massive stupas including Ruwanwelisaya, Jetavanaramaya (one of the tallest brick structures in the ancient world), and Abhayagiri.

Decline (11th Century)

Repeated invasions from South India led to the abandonment of Anuradhapura as the capital. The city was reclaimed by jungle until its rediscovery and restoration in the 19th century.

Sacred Sites & Monuments

Sri Maha Bodhi Tree

The oldest historically documented tree in the world (planted 288 BC). Grown from a cutting of the original Bodhi tree in India.

Ruwanwelisaya Stupa

Built by King Dutugemunu (2nd century BC). White dome stupa standing 103 meters tall with a circumference of 290 meters.

Jetavanaramaya

Once the tallest stupa in the ancient world (122 meters). Built in 3rd century AD, it was the third tallest structure globally at the time.

Twin Ponds (Kuttam Pokuna)

Sophisticated bathing pools for monks with advanced filtration systems. Perfect example of ancient hydraulic engineering.

Abhayagiri Monastery

Once housed 5,000 monks. The monastery complex covered 235 hectares with libraries, hospitals, and meditation halls.

Samadhi Buddha Statue

Serene 4th-century Buddha statue depicting deep meditation. Considered one of the finest Buddha statues in Sri Lanka.

Basawakkulama Tank

Ancient reservoir built by King Pandukabhaya in 4th century BC. Part of sophisticated irrigation network.

Moonstone Carvings

Semicircular carved stones at building entrances depicting Buddhist cosmology and the path to enlightenment.

Religious & Cultural Importance

Anuradhapura remains deeply significant to Buddhism and Sri Lankan culture:

  • Living Sacred City: Still an active pilgrimage site visited by thousands of Buddhist devotees daily
  • Vesak Celebrations: Major Buddhist festival celebrations attract pilgrims from around the world
  • Archaeological Treasure: Ongoing excavations continue to reveal new insights into ancient civilization
  • UNESCO Recognition: Protected as cultural property of outstanding universal value
